Philosophy of Stoics
Ever heard of the phrase "No pain, no gain"? Stoicism at it's core is about the endurance of pain and through that endurance and only that endurance, one will gain strength.
The Three Great Stoics
Epictetus was a Greek philosopher born into slavery best known for his work "Discourses" and "Enchiridion"
Seneca was a Roman philosopher best known for his work "Letters from a Stoic"
Marcus Aurelius was a Roman Emperor who was the last of The Five Great Emperors. He is known for "Meditations"
'It is my good luck that, although this has happened to me, I can bear it without pain, neither crushed by the present nor fearful of the future.' Because such a thing could have happened to any man, but not every man could have borne it without pain. So why see more misfortune in the event than good fortune in your ability to bear it?
-Marcus Aurelius
